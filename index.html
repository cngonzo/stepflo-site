Why users get stuck

Products rarely lose users because they’re impossible. They lose them because the guidance around them is scattered, stale, or arrives too late.

A small story:

A PM ships a new “workspace permissions” feature. The announcement goes out. A help center article is drafted. A quick Loom is recorded. Support gets a one-pager in Slack.

Two weeks later, users still ask the same questions: “Where do I turn this on?” · “Why can’t I see this?” · “Who has access?”

Nothing is technically “wrong.” The knowledge exists. It’s just not showing up in the right place, at the right moment, in the right format.

StepFlo is built to fix exactly that.

What StepFlo does

StepFlo reads the product knowledge you already have and restructures it into clear, reusable steps. No new authoring tool. No separate content universe.

  • Pull from PRDs, specs, and internal docs.
  • Use designs and flows to understand context.
  • Turn it all into accurate, step-by-step guidance.

One place to define the “right way” to use a feature. Many places it can show up to help.

Where the steps go

StepFlo is designed for the channels that actually touch your users and your teams:

  • Inline guidance in your product.
  • Help center and documentation.
  • Onboarding checklists and in-app tours.
  • LMS modules and internal training.
  • Support macros and internal playbooks.

One source of truth. No more rewriting the same explanation five different ways.

Why this matters

  • Faster adoption. Users understand new features when they first encounter them.
  • Less repetition. Support spends less time answering “where do I…?”
  • Clear ownership. PMs and education teams define the steps once, with confidence.
  • Consistent messaging. The same explanation shows up across product, docs, and training.
